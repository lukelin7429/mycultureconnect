UN Youth Climate Ambassador

Edward Huang

Member of UNFCCC YOUNGO, International Ambassador for LCOY Taiwan, and a UCLA graduate. Through My Culture Connect, Edward opens real international stages for Taiwan's students — UN climate summits, SDG teaching, and cross-border project learning. As founder of Youth Network, he secured Taiwan's right to host the UN's LCOY Taiwan in 2026.

Biography

From California to the UN, from Taiwan to the global youth stage

Edward Huang is based in Irvine, California and holds a Master's degree from UCLA. He is a member of YOUNGO, the official Children and Youth Constituency of the UNFCCC, and has been active in global youth climate action since 2023 — taking part in international initiatives such as the global Conference of Youth (COY).

As International Ambassador for LCOY Taiwan and a youth coach, he guides students in connecting their own interests with the UN Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs), amplifying Taiwan's visibility and impact in global climate action through the voices of young people.

A quick glossary

Read the acronyms, understand the stage

The UN-system acronyms that come up most in Edward's work.

UNFCCC

UN Framework Convention on Climate Change

The UN's core climate treaty body.

YOUNGO

Official Youth Constituency of the UNFCCC

The UN climate body's official youth voice.

COP

Conference of the Parties

The annual UN climate summit.

COY

Conference of Youth

The official youth summit just before COP.

LCOY

Local Conference of Youth

A national youth climate forum feeding into COY.

SDGs

Sustainable Development Goals

The UN's 17 global goals to 2030.
