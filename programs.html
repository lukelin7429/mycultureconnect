In loving memory

Grandpa Mike

Long before Dom, the school tour was carried by one extraordinary American volunteer — Michael “Grandpa Mike” Dishnow, who made five self-funded visits to Taiwan and touched countless schools. He passed away in 2025; his kindness still travels through every classroom he loved.

Michael Dishnow — a former guidance counselor and school principal in the United States — began more than a decade ago by coaching Changhua’s children to speak English through video calls, at a time when the whole county had only about six foreign teachers.

When he made his first self-funded visit, students and teachers felt they already knew him. Schools welcomed him with performances, singing, dancing — even lion dances. He embraced Taiwan in return: he studied Chinese, kept records of every friend’s name and address, and faithfully mailed handwritten calligraphy and cards from the US every Lunar New Year.

Back home he taught virtual classes five days a week, wrote articles urging Americans to volunteer in Taiwan, and over ten years shipped boxes of cards, pens, and T-shirts printed “Learn English with Grandpa Mike.” He traveled without complaint to schools as far away as Penghu and Pingtung.

“He taught English, but more importantly, he taught love, respect, and the power of doing good.”
1

Unwavering character

Humble, kind, endlessly encouraging, and quick with a wonderful sense of humor — he never promoted himself.

2

Dedicated advocacy

Taiwan’s tireless spokesperson in the US — writing books and articles and giving his own resources to support Taiwanese schools.

3

Philosophy of action

“People change their views because of your actions, not because of what you say.” He lived it every day.

4

Tireless service

From Changhua to Penghu and Pingtung, he went wherever students needed him — without complaint.

The tour continues · Today

Dom Jones

From Compton to Changhua

Real English, global stories, and a message every student can carry forward.

Dom Jones is an internationally recognized social advocate, educator, and media personality from California. Since December 2025, she has partnered with My Culture Connect to bring a school assembly tour to Changhua County.

Each campus visit gives students the chance to hear living English from an American speaker, connect their local school to the wider world, and reflect on kindness, courage, and the value of their own voice.

"Be the love you did not receive."

Three commitments

What Dom brings to every assembly

01

English Education

Students hear natural, lived English from a working American media personality and respond in a real assembly setting.

02

Global Connections

Dom connects Changhua classrooms with Los Angeles, the United Nations, and the wider world students are preparing to enter.

03

Kindness

Every visit centers empathy, unity, and the belief that every student's voice and dreams matter.
